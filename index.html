Step 1

Who are the main players?

The global financial system is a network of people and organisations that save, borrow, lend, invest, and regulate money.

Households

Earn wages, spend money, save in banks, and borrow for homes or education.

Businesses

Sell goods and services, pay workers, invest, and sometimes borrow to grow.

Banks

Keep deposits safe, make loans, and help money move between people and firms.

Governments

Collect taxes, spend on services, borrow through bonds, and make rules.

Step 3

National vs global finance

Some money activities happen inside one country. Others connect the whole world.

Inside a country

  • People deposit money in local banks
  • Governments collect taxes and spend on services
  • Central banks influence interest rates

Across countries

  • Businesses buy and sell in global markets
  • Investors move money between countries
  • Currencies are exchanged every day

Global organisations

These groups try to support stability, development, and cooperation.

World Bank
